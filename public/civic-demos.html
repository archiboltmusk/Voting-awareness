The Bengal Reader's Civic-Tech Prototypes

Three early-stage tools built to make government data and civic complaints harder to ignore: a registry for broken public infrastructure, a pipeline for turning RTI responses into investigative stories, and a peer-learning guild that trains volunteers by having them build these very tools.

All three are prototypes. The screens below show sample data — read on for what's real and what isn't. For the reasoning behind this project, see Civic Lab.

SwarmAudit — Civic Failure Registry

A geo-tagged registry where residents log broken roads, blocked drains, non-functional health centres, and other civic failures, with photos and location data attached to each report.

Prototype · mock data
What it is: SwarmAudit's idea is simple — a single broken streetlight complaint is easy for a municipal office to ignore. A geo-tagged, timestamped, dated log of hundreds of similar complaints across a ward is harder to. The concept is to compile citizen reports into a public map and a ward-by-ward "Civic Failure Index," and share that log with local authorities on a regular schedule under the Right to Information Act, 2005.

Status: The submission form, interactive map, and database backend described on the full SwarmAudit page are not yet connected to a live database. Everything shown below — the report cards and the index table — is illustrative sample data, not real citizen submissions. Reports are not currently being sent to any municipal office.

RTI Swarm — Data Journalism Pipeline

A structured pipeline for filing Right to Information requests at scale, tracking responses against deadlines, flagging financial discrepancies, and converting raw government disclosures into plain-language explainers.

Prototype · mock data
What it is: RTI Swarm is conceived as three things bundled together: (1) a library of ready-to-edit RTI application templates for common civic questions — school attendance vs. salary records, road repair tenders, hospital medicine stock, and similar; (2) a tracker for filed RTIs showing filing dates, response deadlines, and any discrepancies found between what was allocated and what was actually spent; and (3) a "departmental transparency score" ranking government offices by how often they respond on time.

Status: No RTIs have actually been filed through this tool. The template library text is modelled on real RTI applications under the RTI Act, 2005, but the tracker below, the discrepancy figures, and the department rankings are all sample data for demonstration. Always consult a legal expert before filing a real RTI.

Resilient Skill Guild

A peer-learning programme where volunteers learn Python, UI/UX design, technical writing, video production, or DevOps by building real features for the other two tools on this page, in mentor-guided six-week cohorts.

Prototype · mock data
What it is: The Skill Guild's premise is "learn by shipping" — instead of a generic course, each track's six-week curriculum ends with a real contribution to SwarmAudit or RTI Swarm: a scraper script, a redesigned filter UI, a contributor guide, an explainer video, or a CI/CD pipeline. Volunteers apply via a waitlist form, get matched to a cohort on Discord, and work through weekly sprints with a named mentor.

Status: This is a proposed programme, not a registered institution, and it does not issue accredited qualifications. No cohorts are currently running. The tracks, cohort boards, mentor bios, and graduate outcomes shown below are illustrative of the intended structure, not real enrolment data. The waitlist form on the full page is not yet connected to a live backend.
