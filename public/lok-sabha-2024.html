Vote Share vs Seat Share

State-by-State Highlights

Selected states that defined the 2024 outcome. Seat totals from ECI declared results.

Uttar Pradesh
80 seats — India's largest delegation
SP: 37   INC: 6   BJP: 33
BJP's worst UP result since 2009. SP+INC together won more seats than BJP for the first time since 2012. Ayodhya's Faizabad constituency fell to SP — a symbolic shock.
Maharashtra
48 seats
MVA (INC+SS-UBT+NCP-SP): 30
MahaYuti (BJP+SS-Shinde+NCP-Ajit): 17
Six months before state elections, voters punished the ruling coalition. MVA swept Mumbai. Nagpur — Modi's constituency — held but with a reduced margin.
Tamil Nadu
39 seats
DMK+INDIA bloc: 39
NDA: 0
BJP failed to open its Tamil Nadu account for the third consecutive Lok Sabha election. AIADMK contested alone and was shut out.
West Bengal
42 seats
AITC (TMC): 29   BJP: 12   INC: 1
TMC recovered strongly from its 2021 Lok Sabha dip (18 seats in 2019 vs 22 in 2019, 29 now). BJP lost seats it had held since 2019, partly explaining Suvendu Adhikari's pivot to state-level consolidation.
Bihar
40 seats
NDA (BJP+JDU+LJP-RV): 30
INDIA: 9
JDU's 12 seats in Bihar made Nitish Kumar a critical kingmaker. After switching alliances three times since 2014, Nitish chose NDA and extracted major flood-relief and special-package concessions.
Andhra Pradesh
25 seats
TDP+JSP+BJP (NDA): 21
YSRCP: 4
YSRCP collapsed from 22 seats in 2019 to 4. TDP returned to NDA after a 6-year gap; Chandrababu Naidu's 16 TDP seats made him the second-biggest NDA ally and AP's Special Category Status demand became a coalition condition.
Rajasthan
25 seats
BJP: 14   INC: 8   Others: 3
INC's recovery in Rajasthan — it won only 0 seats here in 2019 — was one of the clearest indicators of anti-incumbency against BJP state governments.
Odisha
21 seats
BJP: 20   BJD: 0
BJD (Naveen Patnaik's party) was swept away in both the Lok Sabha and simultaneous state assembly elections. BJP won its first ever Odisha government after 24 years of BJD rule. A non-aligned regional giant was eliminated.
Kerala
20 seats
UDF (INC+): 18   LDF (CPM+): 1   BJP: 1
BJP won its first ever Kerala Lok Sabha seat (Thrissur), ending a 60-year shut-out. UDF swept the rest in an anti-incumbency wave against the Pinarayi Vijayan state government.
Three turning points in a single election. First: BJP lost its solo majority — the "4-D guarantee" (development, demand-driven growth, democracy, delivery) framing failed to insulate against unemployment and inflation. Second: the Samajwadi Party's UP sweep restored regional socialist politics as a national force. Third: the INDIA alliance held together long enough to nearly double the opposition's 2019 seat count, demonstrating that coalition arithmetic can constrain a dominant party without defeating it outright.
